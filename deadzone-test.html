Deadzone Test

See how far a stick moves before you want games to respond.

Deadzone is the small center area where tiny stick movement is ignored. This test overlays a live deadzone ring on each stick and helps you find a value that masks idle noise without eating too much precise input.

Practical range

Healthy controllers often feel good with deadzones around 0.05 to 0.12, but worn sticks may need more.

Goal

Choose the lowest value that keeps the stick stable at rest. Too much deadzone can make aim and movement feel sluggish.

How to choose a good deadzone

The best setting is small enough to keep input sharp, but large enough to cover idle stick noise.

Start low

Begin around 0.05. If the stick remains stable at rest, you can usually keep the deadzone there or close to it.

Raise only when needed

If the cursor or in-game camera drifts when untouched, increase the value until the live stick point stays centered at rest.

Retest after long play sessions

Heat, wear, and dirt can change how a stick behaves over time, so it helps to recheck controllers that feel inconsistent.
