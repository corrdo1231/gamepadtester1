Controller Drift Test

Measure unwanted stick movement while the controller rests.

This page focuses on stick drift. Leave both analog sticks untouched, reset the drift sample, and watch the live magnitude plus peak idle offset values. If either stick remains outside your deadzone while untouched, you may be looking at drift.

Tip

Test on a flat surface. Small offsets can come from your hand resting on the stick, especially on lighter thumbsticks.

What to watch

Keep an eye on both the current magnitude and the peak idle offset. Repeated values above about 0.10 often feel noticeable in games.

Drift testing tips

These checks help you separate real drift from normal stick noise and hand contact.

Reset before each reading

Clear the peak values, then leave the controller untouched for three to five seconds to capture a cleaner idle sample.

Check both sticks

Left stick drift is more common, but right stick issues still matter for camera control and fine aiming.

Compare against game settings

If the suggested deadzone here matches or exceeds the deadzone you need in games, the controller may need maintenance.
